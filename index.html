methodologyTHE MOST MAHJONG-OBSESSED STATES: METHODOLOGY OBJECTIVE This study ranks all 50 U.S. states and the District of Columbia by their relative level of engagement with the game of mahjong. Because no single statistic captures "obsession," the ranking is built as a composite Mahjong Index that combines behavioral and demographic signals into a single, comparable 0–100 score. DATA SOURCES Four metrics were collected for each state from publicly available sources in April 2026: • Venues & Places to Play: BAM Good Time — Clubs directory (bamgoodtime.com/clubs) • "Mahjong" Avg Search Interest (Past 5 Years): Google Trends (trends.google.com). Values are pre-published on a 0–100 relative scale. • Asian Population (2024, %): World Population Review (worldpopulationreview.com/state-rankings/asian-population) • Senior Population (2023, %): CarePatrol (carepatrol.com/blog/senior-population-by-state/) NORMALIZATION Because the raw metrics use incompatible units (counts, indices, and percentages), each is rescaled to a common 0–100 range using min-max normalization: Normalized = (Value − Min) / (Max − Min) * 100 After this step, the state with the lowest value scores 0, the highest scores 100, and all others fall proportionally between them. WEIGHTED COMPOSITE INDEX Metrics were combined based on their evidentiary weight: • 40% — Search Interest: Strongest observable signal of active public interest across the state. • 30% — Venues & Places to Play: Direct behavioral evidence representing real locations where people gather. • 20% — Asian Population %: Cultural proxy reflecting mahjong's historical and ongoing roots. • 10% — Senior Population %: Demographic proxy (mahjong skews older, but most seniors don't play). Calculation: Composite = (0.40 * Search) + (0.30 * Venues) + (0.20 * Asian%) + (0.10 * Senior%) FINAL MAHJONG INDEX The weighted composite is re-normalized one final time using min-max scaling to span the full 0–100 range. This presentation step anchors the top state at 100 and the bottom state at 0, making it easier to interpret without changing the rank order. PROCESSING PIPELINE All calculations were performed in Python (pandas) for all 51 jurisdictions: 1. Load raw state-level data. 2. Min-max normalize each metric independently to 0–100. 3. Compute the weighted composite. 4. Re-normalize the composite to 0–100 for the final Mahjong Index. 5. Rank jurisdictions in descending order. LIMITATIONS • Venues reflect only the BAM Good Time directory, undercounting informal home games and unregistered community centers. • Google Trends is relative; small states with niche interest can score higher than large states with broader but diluted activity. • Asian population share is a cultural proxy. It does not measure actual players, distinguishing between ethnic traditions, or capture the non-Asian American player base. • Senior population share is a rough proxy; most seniors do not play, and younger audiences are growing. • The 40/30/20/10 weighting is a modeling decision; different defensible weights would slightly reorder the middle of the rankings.
