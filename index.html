A few years ago, you might have associated mahjong exclusively with smoky parlors in Hong Kong or quiet community centers in Florida. But the ancient tile-based game is currently experiencing a massive cultural resurgence across America. With a growing community of players and a surge in stylish, modern mahjong sets, the game has moved from niche hobby to a nationwide obsession—but where is the mahjong capital of the USA?

We looked at all 50 states and D.C. and ranked them on a **Mahjong Popularity Index**. Our rating is based on four key metrics: the number of registered mahjong venues, Google search volume interest over the past five years, and the concentration of the sport’s key demographic groups: Asian American and senior populations.

Most Popular States for Mahjong

Curious to see if your area is a hub for "Pungs" and "Chows"? Here are highlights from the most obsessed U.S. states when it comes to mahjong, according to our composite index:

1. Florida (Index Score: 100.00)

Florida is the U.S. state most obsessed with mahjong. With a perfect indexed score, it reigns supreme. Florida boasts a massive **88 registered venues**, largely driven by its high senior population (**21.74%**), a demographic that has long kept the American version of the game alive in social clubs and retirement communities.

2. Pennsylvania (Index Score: 95.06)

Ranking second is Pennsylvania. While it has fewer registered venues than Florida, it shows a remarkably high level of curiosity and digital engagement, scoring a strong **57.16/100** in Google Search interest.

3. California (Index Score: 94.88)

California takes third. Its obsession is fueled by its cultural diversity; it has the largest raw **Asian Population** total in our top five, which serves as a vital cultural anchor for both traditional and modern variants of the game.

Methodology

To find the most mahjong-obsessed states, we created a weighted **Mahjong Index (0–100)** based on four metrics from April 2026 data:

  • **40% - Google Trends (5 Years):** Highest search interest in "Mahjong."
  • **30% - Registered Venues:** Count from the BAM Good Time directory.
  • **20% - Asian Population % (2024):** Cultural proxy.
  • **10% - Senior Population % (2023):** Demographic proxy.

All raw metrics were min-max normalized (0-100) before weighting. The final index was normalized a second time to anchor the top state at exactly 100 and the bottom jurisdiction at exactly 0, allowing for clear comparative scoring.
